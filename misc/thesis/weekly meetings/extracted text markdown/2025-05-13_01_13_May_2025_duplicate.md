## Slide 8

- Concave Hull

- Keep only the outer points that form the boundary, and ensure no outward dents (non-concave angles) exist.

Build a Delaunay triangulation
 This divides the points into triangles so that no point lies inside the circumcircle of any triangle.

Filter triangles using α
 For each triangle:

- Compute the circumradius (radius of the circle that passes through its 3 vertices).

- If the circumradius < 1/α, keep the triangle.

- If it's too large, it's likely spanning over empty space → remove it.

- Form the boundary (polygon)

- The outer edges of the remaining triangles are stitched together to form the concave hull.
