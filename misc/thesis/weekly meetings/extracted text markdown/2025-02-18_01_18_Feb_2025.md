## Slide 11

- Detectron-2

- Developed by Facebook AI Research (FAIR), Detectron2 is a state-of-the-art, object detection library built on PyTorch.

- It supports various tasks like object detection, instance segmentation, keypoint detection,

- Comes with models pre-trained on large datasets (e.g., COCO)

- Typically uses CNNs (like ResNet) to extract features from images.

- Enhances the backbone by combining features at multiple scales, which is particularly useful when objects (like tree crowns) vary in size.

## Slide 13

- Working

- Backbone (Feature Extraction)

What It Is:
A convolutional neural network (CNN) such as ResNet (e.g., ResNet-50 or ResNet-101).

- Role in the Pipeline:

- Feature Extraction: The backbone processes the input image to extract low-level to high-level features (like edges, textures, and more complex patterns).

- Output: It produces feature maps that capture the visual information of the image in a condensed form, setting the stage for further processing.

- FPN (Feature Pyramid Network)

What It Is:
A network component that builds multi-scale feature maps.

- Role in the Pipeline:

- Handling Scale Variations: Objects in images, such as tree crowns, can vary greatly in size. FPN creates a pyramid of feature maps at different scales, ensuring that both small and large objects can be effectively detected.

- Enhancement: It combines features from different layers of the backbone to produce rich, semantically strong feature maps at multiple resolutions.

## Slide 14

- Working

- RPN (Region Proposal Network)

What It Is:
A network that slides over the FPN feature maps to propose candidate regions in the image that are likely to contain objects.

- Role in the Pipeline:

- Region Proposals: The RPN generates multiple bounding box proposals with objectness scores. These proposals are regions of interest that could contain an object (like a tree).

- Filtering: It eliminates unlikely regions, reducing the number of candidate regions to a manageable set for the next stage.

- ROI Heads (Region of Interest Heads: Classification & Refinement)

What It Is:
Components that take the proposed regions from the RPN and process them further.

- Role in the Pipeline:

- ROI Pooling/Align: The candidate regions are resized to a fixed dimension using ROI pooling (or ROI Align), which ensures that they can be processed by the fully connected layers regardless of the region’s original size.

- Classification: The network classifies each region to determine what object it represents (e.g., tree, building, etc.).

- Bounding Box Refinement: It refines the coordinates of the bounding boxes for a more precise localization of the objects.

- Semantic Segmentation for Tree Patches

- Model

- Architecture

- Suitability for Drone Phenology Monitoring

- UNet

- Encoder–decoder with skip connections

- • Lightweight and fast inference with pretrained weights• Good at capturing fine local details

- • May not handle complex or heterogeneous scenes well• Limited global context

- Effective when tree canopies are distinct and separation is clear; great for rapid prototyping without additional training

- DeepLab

- Atrous convolution with spatial pyramid pooling and CRF

- • Pretrained models provide strong multi-scale context and refined boundaries• Robust in varied scenes

- • More computationally intensive than UNet• Slower inference speed could be an issue

- Ideal for scenes with variable canopy sizes and seasonal changes, where detailed boundary detection is critical

- Mask R-CNN

- Two-stage detector (Faster R-CNN backbone) with segmentation branch

- • Offers instance-level segmentation with precise extraction of individual trees• Pretrained weights available

- • More complex pipeline and slower inference even when using pretrained models

- Best when distinguishing overlapping or joined canopies is essential, though it might be overkill for simpler segmentation tasks

## Slide 19

- PointRend (R101)

- It "renders" the segmentation mask by predicting the label for each point in the image where it’s needed

- By drawing an analogy to rendering, the model can focus on areas that need higher resolution—typically object boundaries or regions with complex textures.

- This approach results in more precise segmentation since it can “zoom in” on uncertain or detailed regions rather than relying on a uniform,

- Instead of uniformly processing every pixel, PointRend selects a subset of points for refinement. It starts with a coarse segmentation prediction and then adaptively samples points where the initial prediction is uncertain or likely to be inaccurate (often near edges or boundaries). Adaptive sampling ensures that the model refines predictions only where needed, leading to better handling of fine details without processing the entire image at high resolution.This mask provides a quick, low-resolution estimate of which regions belong to an object

- The point head focuses on the exact location with rich local features, enabling more accurate predictions at a fine level.

- The primary benefit of PointRend is its ability to produce sharp, accurate boundaries by rendering fine details at the object edges. By re-evaluating only the uncertain regions, the model can correct and refine boundaries that are often blurred in coarse segmentation masks.
