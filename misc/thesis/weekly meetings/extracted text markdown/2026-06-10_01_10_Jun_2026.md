## Slide 12

- Histogram Gradient Boosting Classifier

- A random forest builds many independent trees and averages them.

- Gradient boosting builds trees sequentially, where each new tree tries to fix the mistakes of the previous trees.

- Good for nonlinear patterns in tabular data.

- Numeric feature values are grouped into bins, or “histograms”.

- The model learns decision rules using these binned values.
