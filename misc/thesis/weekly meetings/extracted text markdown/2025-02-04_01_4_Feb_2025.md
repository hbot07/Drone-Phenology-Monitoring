## Slide 7

- UTM Coordinates

- UTM coordinates are a type of projected coordinate system that divides the world into 60 north-south zones, each 6° of longitude wide. Coordinates within each zone are given as eastings and northings in meters.

- For converting this to Latitudes and Longitudes in degrees we need to know utm zone.

- We get this from coordinates.txt in the generated om by webodm: WGS84 UTM 43N

- We can use QGIS for conversion, but for automation purposes, pyproj library in python provides Proj, transform methods for conversion.

## Slide 8

- UTM vs Geographic Coordinates

- Feature

- Geographic Coordinates

- UTM Coordinates

- Scale of Use

- Global

- Local to regional within a single UTM zone

- Unit of Measure

- Degrees

- Meters

- Common Uses

- Navigation, global datasets, general communication

- Detailed mapping, surveying, construction

- Precision

- Variable, depends on decimal places used

- High precision within zones, straightforward for distance and area calculations

- Calculations

- More complex for distance and area due to curvature

- Simpler and more accurate for linear measurements

- Distortion

- Can vary significantly, especially near the poles

- Minimal within each zone, designed to reduce distortion

- Ideal Applications

- Broad public reporting, education, general GIS work

- Engineering, land development, precision GIS tasks

- Integration with GPS

- Direct compatibility with global positioning systems

- Typically requires conversion from latitude and longitude

- Visualization

- Globally consistent, no interruptions across zones

- Discontinuities may occur at zone boundaries

- Software and Hardware

- Universally supported in both software and hardware

- May require specific settings or conversion in software
