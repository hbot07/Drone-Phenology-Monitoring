## Slide 20

- Traditional vegetation indices like NDVI often "dip" during flowering because flowers disrupt the chlorophyll signal.

- The Solution: Pigment-specific indices:

Normalized Difference Yellowness Index (NDYI):
NDYI=rho(Green)-rho(blue)/rho(Green)+rho(blue)

- Target: yellow flowers.

- Problem: Sunlight reflecting off shiny waxy leaves (specular glint) can mimic the brightness and color of flowers.

- The Solution: Gray-Level Co-occurrence Matrix (GLCM):

- Energy (Uniformity): Measures the homogeneity of the surface. Flower petals in clusters are structurally more uniform and organized compared to the high-contrast edges of a leaf-and-twig canopy.

- Robustness: By checking for high "Energy" values, the algorithm confirms that a color signal belongs to a smooth floral surface rather than a jagged vegetative one.
