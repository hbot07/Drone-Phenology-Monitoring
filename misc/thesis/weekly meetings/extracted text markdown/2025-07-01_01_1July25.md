## Slide 2

- Objective

- Given two sets of crown polygons (OM3 and OM4)

- OM3 is misaligned with respect to OM4

- Goal: Affinely align OM3 to OM4 to maximize overlap (IoU)

- Calculate A-B and B-A but with constraint

## Slide 3

- Initial Approach

- Average Translation Vector

For each matched pair, computed shift:
Δx = x_OM4 − x_OM3, Δy = y_OM4 − y_OM3

- Took mean of all shifts

- Applied global translation to OM3 crowns

- Problem:

- Mean is sensitive to outliers (even 1–2 bad matches can shift the average)

## Slide 4

- First Attempt

- Nearest Neighbor Matching

- Computed centroids of all OM3 and OM4 crowns

- Matched each OM3 centroid to its nearest OM4 neighbor

- Outcome:

- Final average IoU: 71%

## Slide 5

- Median Translation Vector

- Recomputed all pairwise shifts between matched centroids

- Took median of all shifts instead of mean

- Median is robust to outliers

- Outcome:

- IoU improved from 71% → ~80%

- Why it works:

- Median ignores extreme outliers and captures central tendency of majority-good matches

## Slide 6

- Final Enhancement

- Filtering Top 15% Shift Outliers

- Calculated L2 norm of each shift vector (magnitude of shift)

- Removed top 15% largest shifts:

- Took median of remaining shifts

- Outcome:

- IoU improved further: 81.34%

- Why this works:

- Removes bad matches entirely (not just suppressing them)

- Median over filtered data → better alignment with actual crown overlaps

## Slide 7

- Post-Alignment

- A−B vs B−A Difference Based on Area

- After alignment, performed polygon-wise IoU matching

- For unmatched or low-overlap crowns:

- Compute A−B and B−A

- Choose the one with larger area
