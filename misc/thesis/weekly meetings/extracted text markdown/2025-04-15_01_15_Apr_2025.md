## Slide 3

- Hungarian Algorithm

- The Hungarian algorithm (also called Kuhn-Munkres algorithm) is a combinatorial optimization method that solves assignment problems in O(n³) time complexity.

- The algorithm operates on a cost matrix representing assignment costs between two sets (e.g., tree detections in consecutive drone flights). It finds the optimal one-to-one matching that minimizes total cost

## Slide 4

- Hungarian Algorithm

Cost Matrix Construction
Create matrix where entry (i,j) represents the Euclidean distance between:

- Polygon i's centroid in orthomosaic t

- Polygon j's centroid in orthomosaic t+1

## Slide 14

- Precision: The proportion of detected trees that are actual trees

- Recall: The proportion of actual trees that were detected

- F1 Score: The harmonic mean

- Omission Error: The percentage of actual trees missed by the detection (100% - Recall)

- Commission Error: The percentage of detected objects that are not actually trees
