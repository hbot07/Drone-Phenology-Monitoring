## Slide 6

- Cost Metric of Hungarian

- Metric

- Type

- Pros

- Cons

- Euclidean Distance

- Centroid-based

- - Simple computation

- - Effective for small positional shifts

- - Ignores box size/overlap

- - Fails on split detections

- IoU

- Overlap-based

- - Directly measures detection quality

- - Penalizes splits/merges

- - Computationally heavier

- - Sensitive to box alignment

- Combined Metric

- Hybrid

- - Balances position and overlap (e.g., λ*IoU + (1-λ)*Euclidean)

- - Requires weight tuning

## Slide 7

- Implementation of Hungarian

- Construct an M×N cost matrix where

- C₍ᵢⱼ₎ = 1 – IoU(Aᵢ, Bⱼ) (perfect overlap→0, no overlap→1)

- Feed that NumPy matrix into SciPy’s linear_sum_assignment (Hungarian) to get optimal row/column pairs

- SciPy auto‑pads rectangular matrices to square; any dummy assignments are ignored in our match list

- Guarantees a global minimum of ∑(1–IoU) across all matches
