## Slide 17

- Flower Detection work

- Initial Goal: Detect flowering trees using standard RGB thresholds (High RCC/GCC).

- First we did: Successfully identified flowering vs. non-flowering canopies.

- The Problem that emerged: Standard "Red" detection was picking up Yellow-flowering trees.

- We found: Yellow petals (Red + Green) trigger the same "Red" sensors as Semal flowers, causing significant false positives.

- implemented the VARI Index to solve the first-order problem: distinguishing healthy leaves from any flower.

- Threshold: VARI > 0.12 = "Green" (Bypasses flower logic).

- Justification: Exploits the deciduous nature of Semal (blooming on bare wood).

- Source: Gitelson et al. (2002); Phenological timing of Bombax ceiba.

## Slide 18

- New Algorithm: A Hierarchical Priority System.

- The Logic: Test for Yellow first using NDYI.

- The Red-Green Gap: Established a strict constraint where Red (Semal) is only confirmed if Green (GCC) is below 0.28.

- Result: Mathematically excludes the "Green" component inherent in yellow flowers.

- Source: RGB Color Space Theory and Spectral Gap Analysis.

- Formulas we used:

- NDYI: (G-B)/(G+B) — Targets yellow petals via blue-light absorption.

- VARI: (G-R)/(G+R-B) — Robust greenness indicator for crown filtering.

- RCC/GCC: Chromatic coordinates used to measure band dominance.

- Source: Sulik & Long (2016); Frontiers in Plant Science (UAV Phenology).

## Slide 19

- Method: Crown is subdivided into a 10 *10 grid. Each cell votes independently.

- Result: Final classification requires a > 45% agreement .

- What it does: Resolves "Salt and Pepper" noise and "Weak Semal" (Orange) misclassifications caused by sun glints.

- Source: Object-Based Image Analysis (OBIA) principles for high-res imagery.
